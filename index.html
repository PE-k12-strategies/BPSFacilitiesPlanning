Welcome to the Brevard Public Schools District Exploration Dashboard!

The dashboard lets you learn about existing conditions at every school, test future scenarios, and visualize these layers on an interactive district map.

The educational marketplace is changing to include options beyond traditional public schools. The school age population in Brevard County is also changing. As such, Brevard Public Schools are experiencing changes in enrollment patterns that have resulted in some schools becoming under-enrolled and some schools over-enrolled. Projections indicate these trends will likely continue.

In order to best serve students and the community, Brevard Public Schools has undertaken an analysis of school enrollment and demographic patterns that will allow the District to make strategic decisions to use facility and financial resources as effectively as possible.

As an initial step in this process, the District has engaged Perkins Eastman to develop an interactive web tool to display and analyze enrollment and demographic data. You are invited to explore the tool, review the data, and build scenarios that could more efficiently utilize school facilities.

You are also invited to take a brief survey about educational preferences that will help shape the future of school facilities in Brevard. The web tool and the survey will be open through Fall 2026, after which the project team will compile the data and present the results to the community.

How to use(click to collapse)
  • This page allows you to explore different ways schools could be combined, grade levels could be redistributed, or school boundaries could be changed.
  • Each school is designed to fit a certain number of students (capacity), so these changes could balance the total number of students at each school (enrollment) to make sure there are the right number of students for the number of available seats (utilization).
  • Pick a base school from the dropdown below to load its profile and nearby schools which could be consolidated with it.
  • Check these nearby schools on or off (either the full school, or just the grade levels you choose) to see what would happen to enrollment and utilization if these students attended the base school instead.
  • Turn on Limit to existing feeder chains to only show the schools that already send students to the base school or receive students from it.
  • Scroll down to view the demographics of this merged school scenario. Explore student locations and travel distances on the map.
  • Use the buttons above to reset or save and share your scenario.

    How to use(click to collapse)
    1. This page lets you explore how school boundaries could be changed to better align the number of students at each school to the number of available seats (utilization). These changes could also shorten how far students travel to school, or match school boundaries better to real neighborhoods.
    2. Add up to five boundaries, either starting from a school’s existing boundary or starting from scratch. Each boundary gets its own color and title, which you can rename, and you can click on the colors to switch the active school boundary.
    3. Choose a base school (optional), and/or use the map tools below to click and drag areas on the map to add or erase them from your boundary. Each small hexagon is a neighborhood-sized area (0.25mi wide, ~25 acres) where one or multiple students live. We group the data this way to protect student privacy.
    4. Pick which grade levels each boundary covers. For example, choose only grades 7 and 8 for a middle school. Boundaries can only overlap on the map if they do not share any of the same grades.
    5. Click “+ Add Boundary” to start the next one, up to five schools total. You can go back to an earlier boundary at any time. Use “Clear active boundary” to reset your selection, or use “Reset scenario” (above) to start your whole map over.
    6. Statistics for the active boundary are shown below. The summary table at the bottom of the page compares enrollment, seat capacity, and utilization across all of your boundaries.
    7. Click “Save and Share Scenario” to email a PDF, a short summary, and a link to your work — to yourself and/or to the project team.
